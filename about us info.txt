About Sankofa

Sankofa Alkebulan University is an independent intellectual and educational initiative dedicated to advancing African knowledge systems, critical scholarship, and transformative research. The institution is founded on the philosophical principle of Sankofa, an Akan concept from West Africa which means “to go back and retrieve what has been lost.” This philosophy expresses the belief that societies must draw wisdom from their past in order to build a stronger and more enlightened future.

The university seeks to cultivate a scholarly environment where African history, philosophy, spirituality, governance traditions, and indigenous knowledge are studied with intellectual rigor and dignity. Through research, writing, dialogue, and academic collaboration, Sankofa Alkebulan University promotes the recovery and reinterpretation of African intellectual heritage that has often been marginalized in global academic discourse.

The institution functions as a platform for multidisciplinary scholarship. It encourages work that bridges theology, philosophy, social sciences, political analysis, and cultural studies, with a particular focus on African realities and global interconnectedness. Scholars affiliated with the university pursue research that addresses contemporary African challenges while grounding their inquiry in historical memory and cultural wisdom.

Sankofa Alkebulan University also aims to nurture a community of thinkers committed to ethical leadership, intellectual independence, and public engagement. Its programs and initiatives emphasize the responsibility of scholars to contribute meaningfully to society by producing knowledge that informs public policy, social transformation, and cultural renewal.

Guided by the principle that knowledge must serve both truth and human flourishing, Sankofa Alkebulan University positions itself as a center for reflective scholarship and a voice for the revitalization of African intellectual traditions in the modern world.
History of Sankofa

Sankofa Alkebulan University was established as an intellectual and academic initiative grounded in the conviction that Africa’s future must be built upon a conscious recovery of its intellectual, spiritual, and cultural heritage. The institution draws its inspiration from the Akan philosophical concept of Sankofa, a principle that urges societies to return to their historical memory in order to retrieve knowledge that can guide present and future development.

The origins of the university lie in a broader intellectual movement seeking to reclaim African knowledge traditions that were marginalized during the colonial and post-colonial eras. For many generations, formal educational institutions across the continent largely emphasized external epistemologies while giving limited attention to Africa’s own philosophical systems, indigenous sciences, governance traditions, and theological reflections. Sankofa Alkebulan University emerged as part of an effort to address this imbalance by creating a scholarly platform where African thought could be studied, preserved, and developed with academic rigor.

From its early conceptual stages, the institution envisioned itself not merely as a traditional educational establishment but as a center for intellectual restoration. Scholars, researchers, and writers associated with the initiative sought to create a space where interdisciplinary inquiry could flourish—bringing together history, philosophy, theology, political studies, cultural analysis, and indigenous knowledge systems. The goal was to cultivate a community of scholars capable of engaging Africa’s contemporary challenges while remaining deeply grounded in the continent’s historical wisdom.

Over time, the vision of Sankofa Alkebulan University expanded to include broader objectives such as promoting scholarly publication, encouraging independent research, facilitating public intellectual dialogue, and supporting initiatives that strengthen African intellectual sovereignty. Through its activities, the institution continues to pursue the recovery of forgotten narratives, the reinterpretation of African philosophical traditions, and the development of new frameworks for understanding Africa’s place in the modern world.

Today, Sankofa Alkebulan University stands as a developing intellectual platform committed to advancing African scholarship, strengthening academic collaboration, and contributing to the global conversation on knowledge, identity, and cultural renewal.
Governance Structure

The governance system of Sankofa Alkebulan University is designed to promote institutional integrity, academic excellence, and responsible leadership. The university operates through a multi-layered governance framework that combines visionary leadership, policy oversight, academic authority, and executive administration. This structure ensures that strategic guidance, academic standards, and operational management remain clearly defined and effectively coordinated.

The governance model is organized around several principal bodies and offices, each with distinct responsibilities that collectively guide the university’s development and mission.


---

The Founder is the originator of the university and the principal custodian of its philosophical vision and institutional mission. The Founder provides intellectual direction, safeguards the founding principles of the university, and ensures that the institution remains faithful to its core purpose of advancing African knowledge systems and scholarly inquiry.

While the Founder does not necessarily engage in day-to-day administration, the office carries symbolic and strategic authority in shaping the long-term direction of the institution and preserving its founding ethos.


---

Ensuring compliance with legal and ethical standards

Protecting the mission and institutional integrity of the university

The Board of Governance functions as the guardian of the institution’s long-term stability and credibility.

Charter of Sankofa Alkebulan University

Preamble

We, the founders and custodians of Sankofa Alkebulan University, recognizing the enduring value of African knowledge systems and the need for rigorous scholarship, hereby establish this Charter as the supreme governing instrument of the University.

Guided by the principle of Sankofa—“return and fetch what is lost”—we affirm the creation of an academic institution devoted to intellectual inquiry, ethical leadership, and the revitalization of African thought within the global academic community.

This Charter establishes the University’s mission, governance, legal authority, academic frameworks, ethical standards, and institutional ceremonies, ensuring alignment with international standards of higher education.


Article I

Establishment and Legal Identity

1. Sankofa Alkebulan University is hereby constituted as an independent, degree-granting university with full legal personality.


2. The University shall operate as a center for multidisciplinary scholarship, research, teaching, and knowledge dissemination.


3. The University is authorized to award undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral degrees in accordance with its statutes and recognized academic standards.


4. The University shall be registered with the relevant national higher education authority and comply with all applicable laws and regulations.


5. The University shall maintain a Seal and official emblem to authenticate all formal documents, certificates, and degrees.


Article II

Motto, Vision, and Mission

Motto: “Knowledge Reclaimed, Wisdom Renewed”

Vision: To become a leading African university renowned for scholarship, intellectual leadership, and the revitalization of African knowledge traditions.

Mission:

1. Advance knowledge through rigorous research, critical inquiry, and innovation.


2. Preserve, interpret, and promote African intellectual traditions.


3. Foster ethical, culturally grounded leadership and citizenship.


4. Engage in global scholarship while centering African perspectives.


5. Contribute to sustainable social, economic, and political development in Africa and beyond.


Article III

Foundational Principles

1. Academic Freedom: Scholars enjoy freedom of research, teaching, and expression within ethical and scholarly boundaries.


2. Intellectual Integrity: All academic work must adhere to the highest standards of honesty, rigor, and reproducibility.


3. African Intellectual Renewal: The University prioritizes the preservation and advancement of African knowledge systems.


4. Ethical Leadership and Responsibility: Scholarship must serve justice, human dignity, and societal transformation.


5. Global Engagement: The University shall engage in international collaborations while maintaining autonomy and African-centered perspectives.


6. Inclusivity and Equity: The University promotes access, diversity, and gender equality in all programs, research, and governance structures.


7. Sustainability: The University integrates environmental stewardship, social responsibility, and ethical finance into all operations.


Article IV

Governance Structure

The University shall be governed by the following bodies:

1. The Founder


2. Board of Governance


3. Advisory Council


4. University Council


5. Academic Senate


6. Executive Directors


7. Academic Offices (Deans, Departments, Research Units)


8. Student Representative Council



Each body shall operate within the authority prescribed by this Charter and the University Statutes, ensuring accountability, transparency, and ethical oversight.


Article V

Founder

1. The Founder is the principal custodian of the University’s vision, mission, and philosophical foundations.


2. The Founder provides strategic guidance and ensures fidelity to the principles of Sankofa.


3. The Founder participates in major deliberations concerning institutional strategy, long-term planning, and academic innovation.


4. The Founder may appoint and recommend members to the Board and Advisory Council.


Article VI

Board of Governance

1. The Board is the supreme supervisory authority of the University.


2. Responsibilities include oversight of finances, strategic planning, institutional sustainability, and major policy approval.


3. The Board shall comprise eminent scholars, professionals, and leaders with proven expertise.


4. The Board shall convene at least twice annually and may establish subcommittees for finance, audit, academic oversight, and risk management.


5. The Board is responsible for compliance with national laws, international accreditation standards, and ethical governance.


Article VII

Advisory Council

1. The Advisory Council consists of distinguished academics, professionals, and thought leaders providing strategic counsel.


2. It advises on academic programs, research priorities, innovation, and international collaborations.


3. The Council serves in a consultative capacity and does not hold executive authority.


4. Council members serve fixed terms of office as defined in the Statutes.


Article VIII

University Council

1. The University Council is responsible for policy implementation, administrative oversight, and institutional planning.


2. It coordinates all University operations in line with the Charter and Statutes.


3. The Council reports to the Board of Governance and liaises with the Academic Senate on academic matters.


4. It ensures operational compliance, risk management, and ethical financial practices.



Article IX

Academic Senate

1. The Academic Senate oversees all academic affairs and safeguards scholarly standards.


2. Responsibilities include:

Approval of curricula, programs, and degree frameworks

Oversight of research ethics, integrity, and compliance

Monitoring teaching quality, faculty recruitment, and promotions

Accreditation and quality assurance of academic programs



3. The Senate ensures adherence to academic freedom, intellectual integrity, and ethical research practices.


4. It convenes at least twice annually and may form specialized committees for curriculum, research, ethics, and student affairs.


Article X

Executive Administration

1. Executive Directors manage day-to-day operations and implement University policies.


2. Academic Offices—including Deans, Departments, and Research Units—coordinate teaching, research, and scholarship.


3. Executive Directors are accountable to the University Council and the Board of Governance.


4. Compliance, risk management, and reporting protocols are embedded in all operational units.



Article XI

Faculties, Schools, and Research Centers

1. The University shall establish Faculties, Schools, and Research Centers to fulfill its academic mission.


2. Initial Faculties may include:

Faculty of African Philosophy and Theology

Faculty of Governance and Public Policy

Faculty of Science and Technology

Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences



3. Each Faculty shall have a Dean, Departments, and designated academic staff, operating under Academic Senate guidance.


4. Research Centers shall focus on strategic areas including African Studies, Sustainable Development, Technology, Global Governance, and Innovation.


5. Faculties and Research Centers must implement programs aligned with sustainable development and Pan-African principles.



Article XII

Student Representation and Welfare

1. Students shall be represented in governance through a Student Representative Council (SRC).


2. The SRC participates in decisions affecting academic policy, student life, and welfare.


3. The University ensures mechanisms for student grievances, counseling, and career guidance.



Article XIII

Ceremonies and Academic Traditions

1. The University shall conduct annual convocation ceremonies to confer degrees.


2. Academic dress and regalia shall be standardized and reflect the University’s heritage and identity.


3. Honorary degrees may be awarded to individuals demonstrating exceptional contributions to scholarship, leadership, or African society.


4. Traditional African cultural elements shall be integrated into institutional ceremonies to reinforce heritage and identity.


Article XIV

Statutes, Policies, and Regulations

1. Statutes and policies shall regulate academic, administrative, financial, and ethical affairs.


2. Statutes must align with the Charter and require approval by the Board, in consultation with the University Council and Academic Senate.


3. Policies cover academic conduct, research ethics, staff recruitment, financial management, and student affairs.


4. Non-compliance with policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including suspension or dismissal.


Article XV

Research Integrity and Ethics

1. All research conducted under the University’s auspices shall adhere to internationally recognized standards of ethics and integrity.


2. Plagiarism, fabrication, and falsification are strictly prohibited.


3. Research involving human participants, animals, or sensitive data requires prior review by an Ethics Committee.


4. Intellectual property arising from research is owned in accordance with University statutes and relevant laws.




---

Article XVI

Amendment of the Charter

1. Amendments may be proposed by the Board of Governance, University Council, or Academic Senate.


2. Amendments require approval by at least two-thirds of the Board and consultation with all relevant academic bodies.


3. No amendment shall compromise the University’s founding mission, vision, or guiding principles.


Article XVII

Authority and Enforcement

1. This Charter is the supreme governing instrument of Sankofa Alkebulan University.


2. All statutes, regulations, and administrative policies derive their authority from this Charter.


3. In any conflict, the provisions of this Charter shall prevail.


Article XVIII

Legal Registration and Recognition

1. The University is legally registered with the Ministry of Higher Education (or equivalent authority).


2. It is authorized to confer degrees, diplomas, and certificates recognized nationally and internationally.


3. All official documents and certifications shall bear the Seal and signature of the University authorities.


Article XIX

Seal, Emblem, and Branding

1. The University Seal authenticates all official acts, degrees, and certifications.


2. The emblem symbolizes the University’s heritage, mission, and African intellectual identity.


3. Branding shall be consistent across all digital, print, and ceremonial communications.


Article XX

Compliance and Accountability

1. The University shall maintain internal audit, compliance, and risk management functions.


2. Annual reporting to the Board and relevant government authorities is mandatory.


3. Independent external audits shall be conducted regularly to ensure transparency and financial integrity.
